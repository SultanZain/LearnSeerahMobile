**Details of the Event:**

The first person to believe in Allah and His Messenger ﷺ was Khadijah bint Khuwaylid, his wife. Then came Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, and Ali ibn Abi Talib. After them, Zayd ibn Harithah, the freed slave of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, embraced Islam.

Through the efforts of Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, the following individuals also embraced Islam: Uthman ibn Affan, az-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam, Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, Talhah ibn Ubaydillah, and Abdur Rahman ibn Awf.

Subsequently, Abu Ubaydah ibn al-Jarrah, Abu Salamah ibn Abd al-Asad, and Uthman ibn Maz'un embraced Islam, followed by his two brothers Qudamah and Abdullah, and his son as-Sa'ib ibn Uthman ibn Maz'un. Asma' bint Abi Bakr al-Siddiq and Aisha bint Abi Bakr al-Siddiq (who was young at the time) also embraced Islam.

Then, Khalid ibn Sa'id ibn al-As and his wife Umaynah bint Khalf ibn As'ad al-Khuza'iyyah, Bilal, Ammar ibn Yasir and his mother Sumayyah, Suhayb ibn Sinan an-Namari (known as ar-Rumi), Amru ibn Abasah as-Sulami, Amru ibn Sa'id ibn al-As, Sa'id ibn Zayd ibn Amru ibn Nufayl and his wife Fatimah bint al-Khattab (the sister of Umar ibn al-Khattab), Umayr ibn Abi Waqqas, Abdullah ibn Mas'ud and his brother Utbah ibn Mas'ud, Sulayt ibn Amru al-Amiri, Ayyash ibn Abi Rabi'ah al-Makhzumi and his wife Asma' bint Salamah ibn Mukharribah at-Tamimiyyah, Mas'ud ibn Rabi'ah ibn Amru al-Qari from Banu al-Hoon ibn Khuzaymah (who were al-Qarah), Khunays ibn Hudhafah ibn Qays ibn Adi as-Sahmi, and Abdullah Jahsh al-Asadi embraced Islam.

**Further Early Believers in the Messenger of Allah ﷺ included:**

Ja'far ibn Abi Talib and his wife Asma' bint Umays, Amir ibn Rabi'ah al-Anazi from Anz ibn Wa'il (an ally of al-Khattab ibn Nufayl), Abu Ahmad ibn Jahsh (the blind), Hatib ibn al-Harith ibn Ma'mar al-Jumahi and his wife (the daughter of al-Mujallil al-Amiriyyah), Hattab ibn al-Harith (his brother) and his wife Fukayhah bint Yasar, their brother Ma'mar ibn al-Harith ibn Ma'mar al-Jumahi, al-Muttalib ibn Azhar ibn Abd Awf az-Zuhri and his wife Ramlah bint Abi Awf as-Sahmiyyah, an-Nahham (whose name was Nu'aym ibn Abdullah al-Adawi), Amir ibn Fuhayrah (a freed slave of Abu Bakr al-Siddiq), Hatib ibn Amru ibn Shams ibn Abd Wudd al-Amiri (the brother of Sulayt ibn Amru), and Abu Hudhayfah ibn Utbah ibn Rabi'ah (whose name was Mihsham ibn Utbah)...
